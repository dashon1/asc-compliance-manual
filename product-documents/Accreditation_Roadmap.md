# ASC Accreditation Roadmap

## 12-Month Compliance Calendar & Deliverable Tracker

**Product:** Ambulatory Surgery Center Compliance Manual — Tier 1  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Applicable Standards:** CMS Conditions for Coverage (42 CFR Part 416), AAAHC Standards, State Licensure Requirements

---

## How to Use This Roadmap

This document provides a month-by-month action plan for achieving and maintaining ASC accreditation. Each month contains:

- **Compliance milestones** tied to CMS and AAAHC requirements
- **Required documentation** you must prepare or update
- **Submission deadlines** and internal due dates
- **Responsible party** assignments for small teams (1–2 compliance staff)

> **Tip for Small Teams:** Block 4–6 hours per week exclusively for compliance work. Treat it like a clinical shift — non-negotiable.

## Month 1: Baseline Assessment & Gap Analysis

### Compliance Milestones
- [ ] Complete facility-wide gap analysis against current CMS Conditions for Coverage
- [ ] Identify all areas of non-compliance or partial compliance
- [ ] Establish compliance committee (even if 2 people — formalize it with meeting minutes)
- [ ] Verify current state licensure status and renewal dates

### Required Documentation
| Document | CMS Reference | Status | Due Date | Owner |
|----------|--------------|--------|----------|-------|
| Gap Analysis Report | §416.41 (Governing Body) | ☐ Not Started | Month 1, Week 2 | Compliance Lead |
| Compliance Committee Charter | §416.41 | ☐ Not Started | Month 1, Week 3 | Administrator |
| Current License Inventory | State Requirements | ☐ Not Started | Month 1, Week 1 | Compliance Lead |
| Organizational Chart | §416.41 | ☐ Not Started | Month 1, Week 2 | Administrator |
| Governing Body Bylaws Review | §416.41 | ☐ Not Started | Month 1, Week 4 | Administrator |

### Submission Deadlines
- **Internal:** Gap analysis complete by end of Week 2
- **External:** None this month (planning phase)
- **State:** Verify licensure renewal date; if within 6 months, begin renewal paperwork immediately

### Action Items for Small Teams
1. Download the latest CMS State Operations Manual, Appendix L (ASC interpretive guidelines)
2. Print AAAHC standards handbook — tab each chapter for quick reference
3. Create a shared digital folder structure mirroring accreditation chapters
4. Schedule recurring weekly 1-hour compliance huddle

## Month 2: Governing Body & Administrative Framework

### Compliance Milestones
- [ ] Establish or update Governing Body structure and meeting schedule
- [ ] Document transfer agreement(s) with local hospital(s) — or physician admission privileges
- [ ] Review and update facility bylaws
- [ ] Verify medical director qualifications and appointment documentation

### Required Documentation
| Document | CMS Reference | Status | Due Date | Owner |
|----------|--------------|--------|----------|-------|
| Governing Body Meeting Minutes (template) | §416.41 | ☐ Not Started | Month 2, Week 1 | Administrator |
| Transfer Agreement (current, signed) | §416.41(b) | ☐ Not Started | Month 2, Week 3 | Administrator |
| Medical Director Credentials File | §416.45 | ☐ Not Started | Month 2, Week 2 | Compliance Lead |
| Bylaws (updated, board-approved) | §416.41 | ☐ Not Started | Month 2, Week 4 | Administrator |
| Disclosure of Ownership (CMS-855B) | §416.41 | ☐ Not Started | Month 2, Week 2 | Administrator |

### Submission Deadlines
- **Internal:** All governing body documents finalized by Week 4
- **External:** Transfer agreement must be executed (signed by both parties) — no CMS deadline but required at survey
- **State:** Check if state requires separate transfer agreement filing

### Critical Notes
> **CMS Hot Button:** Transfer agreements are one of the top 5 deficiency areas. Ensure the agreement is current (not expired), signed by authorized representatives at both the ASC and hospital, and specifies the hospital will accept transfers. If using physician admission privileges instead, maintain current privilege verification letters for every physician on your roster.

### Compliance Milestones
- [ ] Appoint Infection Control Officer (can be dual-role for small ASCs)
- [ ] Develop comprehensive Infection Control Program
- [ ] Establish surveillance, tracking, and reporting protocols
- [ ] Verify sterile processing procedures and documentation
- [ ] Conduct baseline infection control risk assessment

### Required Documentation
| Document | CMS Reference | Status | Due Date | Owner |
|----------|--------------|--------|----------|-------|
| Infection Control Program Manual | §416.51 | ☐ Not Started | Month 4, Week 3 | IC Officer |
| IC Officer Appointment Letter & Qualifications | §416.51(a) | ☐ Not Started | Month 4, Week 1 | Administrator |
| Infection Surveillance Log Template | §416.51(a) | ☐ Not Started | Month 4, Week 2 | IC Officer |

| Hand Hygiene Policy | §416.51 | ☐ Not Started | Month 4, Week 1 | IC Officer |
| Antibiotic Stewardship Policy | §416.51 | ☐ Not Started | Month 4, Week 3 | IC Officer |

### Submission Deadlines
- **Internal:** IC program manual approved by Governing Body by end of Month 4
- **External:** Report any reportable infections to state health department per state timelines
- **CMS:** Infection control is surveyed under §416.51 — one of the highest-cited deficiency areas

### Critical Notes
> **Surveyor Focus Area:** Surveyors WILL observe hand hygiene, PPE use, instrument processing, and environmental cleaning during the on-site visit. They will also request 12 months of surveillance data. Start tracking NOW — even if you have zero infections, document that you have zero infections each month.

| Hazardous Materials Inventory | §416.44 | ☐ Not Started | Month 6, Week 3 | Safety Officer |

### Submission Deadlines
- **Internal:** Emergency preparedness plan approved by Governing Body by Week 4
- **External:** Fire marshal inspection must be current (annually in most states)
- **State:** Verify state-specific life safety requirements — some exceed federal standards

### Critical Notes
> **Survey Day Reality:** Surveyors will physically walk the facility. They check exit signage, fire extinguisher inspection tags, electrical panel clearance (36 inches), crash cart seal integrity, and medication refrigerator temperatures. A single expired fire extinguisher tag can trigger a deficiency. Walk your facility monthly with fresh eyes.

## Month 9: Credentialing & Privileging

### Compliance Milestones
- [ ] Audit all physician and provider credential files
- [ ] Verify current privileging for all providers performing procedures
- [ ] Confirm NPDB query completion (within 2 years)
- [ ] Verify licensure, DEA, malpractice insurance for all providers
- [ ] Update credentialing policies and peer review process

### Required Documentation
| Document | CMS Reference | Status | Due Date | Owner |
|----------|--------------|--------|----------|-------|
| Credentialing Policy | §416.45 | ☐ Not Started | Month 9, Week 2 | Administrator |
| Provider Credential Files (each provider) | §416.45 | ☐ Not Started | Month 9, Week 3 | Compliance Lead |
| Privilege Delineation Forms (by specialty) | §416.45 | ☐ Not Started | Month 9, Week 2 | Medical Director |
| NPDB Query Verification | §416.45 | ☐ Not Started | Month 9, Week 1 | Compliance Lead |
| Peer Review Policy & Minutes | §416.45 | ☐ Not Started | Month 9, Week 3 | Medical Director |
| Malpractice Insurance Verification | §416.45 | ☐ Not Started | Month 9, Week 1 | Compliance Lead |

### Submission Deadlines
- **Internal:** All credential files 100% complete by Month 9, Week 4
- **External:** NPDB queries must be current (within 2 years per AAAHC; CMS expects at initial privileging and reappointment)
- **State:** Verify state credentialing timeline requirements

### Critical Notes
> **#1 Deficiency Nationally:** Credentialing and privileging deficiencies are the most frequently cited issue in ASC surveys. Every single provider file MUST contain: current license, DEA (if applicable), board certification verification, malpractice insurance, NPDB query, privilege delineation, and peer references. Missing ONE document in ONE file = deficiency.
