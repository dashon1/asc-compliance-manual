# Equipment Maintenance Log

## Clinical Device Certification & Maintenance Tracking System

**Product:** Ambulatory Surgery Center Compliance Manual — Tier 1  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Applicable Standards:** CMS §416.44 (Environment), §416.42 (Surgical Services), AAAHC Facility & Equipment Standards, NFPA 99, AAMI Standards

---

## How to Use This Document

This log provides standardized templates for tracking every clinical device in your ASC. CMS requires that all equipment is maintained per manufacturer recommendations and that maintenance records are readily available during survey.

### Instructions
1. **Complete the Master Equipment Inventory** (Section 1) for every device in your facility
2. **Use the Maintenance Event Log** (Section 2) each time maintenance is performed
3. **Track certifications** in the Certification Tracking section (Section 3)
4. **Review the Vendor Directory** (Section 4) to ensure contacts are current
5. **Conduct monthly reviews** using the Monthly Compliance Check (Section 5)

> **Small Team Tip:** Designate one person as "equipment coordinator." Even if they also do clinical work, having a single point of accountability prevents items from falling through the cracks. Schedule 30 minutes on the first Monday of each month for equipment review.

- Assign each device a unique **Asset ID** (e.g., OR1-ANES-001 = OR Room 1, Anesthesia, Unit 001)
- Enter ALL clinical devices, including crash cart contents, sterilization equipment, and monitoring devices
- Update this inventory whenever equipment is added, removed, or relocated

## Section 7: Compliance Quick-Reference

### What Surveyors Look For — Equipment

1. **Maintenance records match manufacturer IFU** — If the manufacturer says annual PM, you must have annual PM documented
2. **No equipment in use past maintenance due date** — If PM is overdue, the device should be taken out of service until serviced
3. **Biological indicator logs for sterilizers** — Weekly minimum, with action documented for any failures
4. **Crash cart sealed and checked daily** — Break-away seal intact, daily check log signed
5. **Temperature logs** — Medication refrigerator, blanket warmer, autoclaves — daily when in use
6. **Emergency generator** — Weekly test log, annual load test, automatic transfer switch test
7. **Medical gas inspection** — Annual third-party inspection report on file
8. **Electrical safety** — Biomedical engineering inspection stickers current on all clinical devices
9. **Manufacturer IFU on file** — For every reprocessed instrument and device
10. **Equipment removed from service is clearly labeled** — "OUT OF SERVICE — DO NOT USE" tags

### Common Deficiencies to Avoid

| Deficiency | How to Prevent |
|-----------|---------------|
| Overdue PM on clinical devices | Set calendar alerts 30 days before PM due dates |
| Missing sterilizer BI documentation | Use standardized log; review weekly |
| Expired defibrillator pads | Add pad expiration to monthly check; stock spares |
| Medication refrigerator temperature gaps | Use continuous monitoring with alarm; backup thermometer |
| No manufacturer IFU on file | Request from manufacturer for every device; file centrally |
| Generator test log gaps | Assign specific staff member; set recurring calendar event |
| Crash cart seal broken without documentation | Policy: every seal break = documented check + reseal |
| Equipment in use without current calibration sticker | Biomedical engineering check stickers visible on each device |
