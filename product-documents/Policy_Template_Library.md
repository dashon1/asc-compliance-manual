## How to Use This Library

Each policy template below includes:
- **Policy statement** and purpose
- **Scope** of who it applies to
- **Key procedure steps** that meet regulatory requirements
- **Documentation requirements**
- **Review schedule**

### Customization Instructions
1. Replace all **[FACILITY NAME]** placeholders with your ASC name
2. Replace **[DATE]** placeholders with the effective date
3. Review each policy against your state-specific requirements
4. Have your Governing Body approve each policy before implementation
5. Train all applicable staff on each policy
6. File the original signed policy and distribute copies as needed

> **Critical:** These templates provide a compliant framework. You MUST customize them to reflect your facility's actual practices, equipment, staffing, and state regulations. A policy that doesn't match what you actually do is worse than no policy — surveyors will cite you for failing to follow your own policies.

#### Purpose
To establish a comprehensive infection prevention and control program that minimizes the risk of healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) to patients, staff, and visitors of **[FACILITY NAME]**.

#### Scope
This policy applies to all staff, credentialed providers, vendors, and visitors within **[FACILITY NAME]**.

#### Definitions
- **HAI:** Healthcare-Associated Infection — an infection acquired during the course of receiving healthcare treatment
- **IC Officer:** The designated individual responsible for the facility's infection control program
- **Standard Precautions:** Infection prevention practices applied to all patient care regardless of suspected or confirmed infection status
- **SSI:** Surgical Site Infection

#### Policy Statement
**[FACILITY NAME]** maintains an active infection prevention and control program that includes surveillance, prevention, and control of infections. The program is overseen by a qualified Infection Control Officer and is reviewed by the Governing Body at least annually.

#### Procedure

**1. Infection Control Officer**
- The IC Officer is appointed by the Governing Body
- Qualifications: Licensed healthcare professional with training/certification in infection control (or actively working toward certification)
- Responsibilities include:
  - Developing and implementing IC policies
  - Conducting infection surveillance
  - Reporting infections per state and federal requirements
  - Providing IC education to staff
  - Conducting annual IC risk assessment
  - Presenting IC data to QAPI committee and Governing Body

**2. Surveillance Program**
- Active surveillance for surgical site infections (SSIs) is conducted for all procedures
- Post-discharge SSI surveillance is conducted for a minimum of 30 days post-procedure (or per CDC/NHSN definitions)
- Surveillance data is collected monthly, analyzed quarterly, and reported to QAPI
- Infection rates are benchmarked against national data (NHSN) when available

**3. Prevention Measures**
- Standard Precautions are followed for all patient encounters
- Transmission-based precautions are implemented when indicated
- Surgical site infection prevention bundles include:
  - Appropriate preoperative skin antisepsis
  - Appropriate antibiotic prophylaxis (per SCIP/current guidelines)
  - Normothermia maintenance
  - Glucose control (diabetic patients)
  - Appropriate hair removal (no razors)
- Environmental cleaning per established schedules and using EPA-registered disinfectants
- Sterile processing per AAMI standards and manufacturer IFU

**4. Reporting**
- Reportable infections are reported to the state health department per state law
- SSI data is reported to NHSN (if participating)
- All infection data is reported to the QAPI committee quarterly
- IC annual report is presented to the Governing Body

**5. Annual Risk Assessment**
- The IC Officer conducts an annual infection control risk assessment
- The risk assessment evaluates: patient population, procedures performed, geographic risks, prior infection data, emerging pathogens, construction/renovation activities
- Priorities are established based on risk assessment findings
- The risk assessment is approved by the Governing Body

#### Documentation
- Infection surveillance logs (maintained monthly)
- IC committee meeting minutes (quarterly minimum)
- Annual IC risk assessment
- IC Officer appointment letter and qualifications
- Staff IC education records
- Reportable infection submissions

#### Review
This policy is reviewed annually by the IC Officer and approved by the Governing Body.

#### Purpose
To reduce the transmission of healthcare-associated pathogens through consistent and proper hand hygiene practices per CDC and WHO guidelines.

#### Scope
All staff, credentialed providers, vendors, and visitors of **[FACILITY NAME]**.

#### Policy Statement
Hand hygiene is the single most effective measure to prevent healthcare-associated infections. All personnel are required to perform hand hygiene per the CDC/WHO 5 Moments of Hand Hygiene.

#### Procedure

**1. Five Moments of Hand Hygiene**
1. Before patient contact
2. Before aseptic task
3. After body fluid exposure risk
4. After patient contact
5. After contact with patient surroundings

**2. Acceptable Methods**
- **Alcohol-based hand rub (ABHR):** Preferred method when hands are not visibly soiled. Apply product, rub all surfaces of hands until dry (minimum 20 seconds)
- **Soap and water:** Required when hands are visibly soiled, after caring for patients with C. difficile or norovirus, and after using the restroom. Wet hands, apply soap, rub all surfaces for minimum 20 seconds, rinse, dry with disposable towel
- **Surgical hand scrub:** Required before all surgical procedures per manufacturer IFU for the surgical scrub product in use

**3. Hand Hygiene Supplies**
- ABHR dispensers are available at all points of care, room entrances/exits, and common areas
- Soap dispensers and paper towels are available at all sinks
- Supplies are checked and restocked daily

**4. Artificial Nails & Jewelry**
- Artificial nails, nail extenders, and nail wraps are prohibited for all staff with direct patient contact
- Natural nails are kept short (< ¼ inch beyond fingertip)
- Ring policy: [FACILITY: define your policy — e.g., plain wedding band only, or no rings in clinical areas]

**5. Monitoring & Compliance**
- Hand hygiene compliance is monitored through direct observation a minimum of quarterly
- Compliance rates are reported to QAPI and IC committee
- Target compliance rate: ≥ 90%
- Non-compliance is addressed through individual counseling and re-education

**6. Education**
- Hand hygiene training is provided at orientation and annually
- Competency includes return demonstration of proper technique

#### Documentation
- Hand hygiene observation audit forms
- Quarterly compliance rates
- Staff training records

## Policy 3: Sterilization & High-Level Disinfection

### [FACILITY NAME] — Policy & Procedure

| Field | Details |

| **Policy Number** | IC-003 |
| **Department** | Sterile Processing / OR |
| **CMS Reference** | 42 CFR §416.51 |
| **Effective Date** | [DATE] |
| **Last Reviewed** | [DATE] |
| **Next Review Due** | [DATE + 12 months] |
| **Approved By** | Governing Body — Date: _________ |

#### Purpose
To ensure all reusable surgical instruments and devices are properly decontaminated, sterilized, or high-level disinfected per manufacturer IFU, AAMI standards, and CDC guidelines.

#### Policy Statement
All reusable critical devices (items that enter sterile tissue) are sterilized before use. All reusable semi-critical devices (items that contact mucous membranes) undergo high-level disinfection (HLD) or sterilization. Processing follows manufacturer IFU, AAMI ST79 (steam sterilization), and AAMI ST91 (flexible endoscope reprocessing) as applicable.

#### Procedure

**1. Decontamination**
- Used instruments are transported to decontamination in closed, labeled containers
- Manual cleaning per manufacturer IFU using appropriate enzymatic detergent
- Automated cleaning (ultrasonic and/or washer-disinfector) per manufacturer IFU
- Visual inspection of each instrument for cleanliness, function, and damage after cleaning

**2. Packaging & Loading**
- Instruments are packaged in FDA-cleared sterilization packaging
- Internal chemical indicator placed in each package
- External chemical indicator visible on outside of each package
- Sterilizer loaded per manufacturer specifications (not overloaded)

**3. Steam Sterilization**
- Sterilization parameters follow manufacturer IFU for the sterilizer and the instruments being processed
- Minimum: 270°F (132°C) for 4 minutes prevacuum, or per specific instrument IFU
- Biological indicator (BI) testing: performed at least weekly and with every implant load
- Chemical indicators (CI): internal and external CI with every load
- Bowie-Dick test (prevacuum sterilizers): daily, before first processed load
- All sterilizer printouts are reviewed, initialed, and filed

**4. Biological Indicator Failure Protocol**
- If a BI test is positive:
  a. Immediately quarantine all items processed since the last negative BI
  b. Notify IC Officer and administration
  c. Remove sterilizer from service
  d. Investigate root cause (operator error, mechanical failure, BI placement)
  e. Run three consecutive negative BIs before returning sterilizer to service
  f. Recall any affected items that were used on patients — notify patients per policy
  g. Document all actions taken

**5. Immediate-Use Steam Sterilization (IUSS / Flash)**
- IUSS is used only when there is an urgent patient need and no other sterile instrument is available
- IUSS is NOT used for implants
- IUSS is NOT used for convenience, routine use, or to compensate for insufficient instrument inventory
- Each IUSS cycle is documented with patient name, reason for IUSS, and cycle parameters
- IUSS usage is tracked and reported to QAPI quarterly

**6. High-Level Disinfection (HLD)**
- Semi-critical devices undergo HLD per manufacturer IFU
- HLD solution concentration is tested before each use (minimum effective concentration — MEC)
- Contact time follows manufacturer IFU (minimum per EPA label)
- Rinsing per IFU with appropriate water quality
- Drying and storage per IFU and AAMI ST91

**7. Single-Use Devices**
- Single-use devices are NOT reprocessed at **[FACILITY NAME]** unless using an FDA-cleared third-party reprocessor
- Single-use devices are discarded after one use

**8. Storage**
- Sterile items are stored in a clean, dry, controlled environment
- Items are stored off the floor, away from ceiling, and in covered/enclosed cabinets
- Event-related sterility: items are considered sterile until the package is compromised
- Staff visually inspect package integrity before use

#### Documentation
- Sterilizer load records (each cycle)
- BI test results (weekly minimum + all implant loads)
- Bowie-Dick test results (daily for prevacuum)
- HLD records (each cycle)
- IUSS usage log
- Equipment maintenance records
- Staff competency records for sterile processing

#### Purpose
To ensure the safe ordering, storage, administration, and monitoring of medications within **[FACILITY NAME]**.

#### Policy Statement
Medications are managed through a safe, organized system that minimizes the risk of medication errors. All medications are administered by qualified, licensed personnel per physician orders.

#### Procedure

**1. Ordering**
- Medications are ordered by authorized prescribers only (licensed and credentialed at the facility)
- Verbal/telephone orders are accepted only by qualified licensed personnel, read back for verification, and authenticated by the prescriber within 48 hours
- Pre-printed order sets are reviewed and approved by the Medical Director annually

**2. Storage**
- Medications are stored per manufacturer specifications
- Refrigerated medications: 36–46°F (2–8°C); temperature logged daily (or continuously monitored with alarm)
- Medications are secured; medication storage areas are locked when unattended
- Look-alike/sound-alike medications are physically separated or flagged
- No medications are stored in unlocked areas accessible to unauthorized personnel

**3. Administration**
- Five Rights verified before every administration: Right Patient, Right Drug, Right Dose, Right Route, Right Time
- Patient identity confirmed with two identifiers before administration
- Allergies verified before administration
- All administered medications are documented immediately in the patient record

**4. Multi-Dose Vials**
- Multi-dose vials are labeled with the date opened
- Multi-dose vials are discarded 28 days after opening (or per manufacturer IFU if shorter)
- Multi-dose vials are dedicated to a single patient whenever possible
- Multi-dose vials are stored per manufacturer IFU (refrigeration if required)

**5. Single-Dose Vials**
- Single-dose vials are used for one patient only and discarded immediately after use
- Single-dose vials are NEVER entered more than once, even for the same patient
- Remaining medication in a single-dose vial is discarded; it is NOT saved for later use

**6. Expiration Monitoring**
- Medication expiration dates are checked monthly (minimum)
- Expired medications are removed immediately and disposed of per policy
- Medications approaching expiration (within 30 days) are flagged

**7. Adverse Drug Events**
- All adverse drug events, medication errors, and near-misses are reported per the Incident Reporting policy
- Significant adverse events are reported to the physician, patient, and QAPI committee
- Root cause analysis is performed for serious medication events

#### Documentation
- Medication administration records (each patient)
- Temperature logs (daily for all medication storage units)
- Monthly expiration check logs
- Controlled substance records (see Policy 5)
- Medication error/adverse event reports
- Pharmacy consultant reports (if applicable)

#### Purpose
To ensure the secure ordering, receipt, storage, administration, documentation, waste, and disposal of controlled substances in compliance with DEA regulations and state pharmacy law.

#### Policy Statement
Controlled substances are managed under strict accountability. All controlled substances are double-locked, counted at each shift change, and discrepancies are investigated immediately.

#### Procedure

**1. Registration & Authorization**
- **[FACILITY NAME]** maintains a current DEA registration (posted in a visible location)
- Only authorized personnel with DEA registration or under the facility's DEA registration may order controlled substances
- State-specific controlled substance licenses are maintained as required

**2. Ordering & Receipt**
- Controlled substances are ordered using DEA Form 222 (Schedule II) or electronic ordering via CSOS
- All orders are received by two authorized personnel
- Received quantities are verified against the order and documented immediately
- Invoice copies are retained for minimum 2 years

**3. Storage**
- All controlled substances are stored in a double-locked system (lock within a lock, or two separate locking mechanisms)
- Keys or access codes are limited to authorized personnel
- Storage areas are not accessible to unauthorized individuals

**4. Administration & Documentation**
- Controlled substances are administered only per physician order
- Administration is documented on the controlled substance log AND the patient record
- Documentation includes: date, time, patient name, drug name, dose, route, administering person, and physician name
- Waste is witnessed by a second licensed person and documented with both signatures

**5. Counting**
- Controlled substance perpetual inventory count is performed at each shift change (minimum) and at the beginning and end of each operative day
- Counts are documented with signature of outgoing and incoming staff
- Any discrepancy triggers immediate investigation

**6. Discrepancy Resolution**
- If a count discrepancy is identified:
  a. Immediate recount by both parties
  b. Review all documentation for the period since last accurate count
  c. Notify the Administrator/designee immediately
  d. If unresolved within 24 hours, notify the DEA (if required) and follow state reporting requirements
  e. Document all investigation steps and outcome
  f. Report to QAPI committee

**7. Waste & Disposal**
- Controlled substance waste is witnessed and co-signed
- Disposal of outdated or unused controlled substances follows DEA regulations (reverse distributor or authorized destruction)
- Disposal is documented and records retained for minimum 2 years

#### Documentation
- Controlled substance perpetual inventory log
- Shift count verification sheets
- DEA Form 222 and invoices
- Waste documentation (dual-signed)
- Discrepancy investigation reports
- DEA registration (current, posted)

## Policy 7: Informed Consent

### [FACILITY NAME] — Policy & Procedure

| **Effective Date** | [DATE] |
| **Last Reviewed** | [DATE] |

| **Approved By** | Governing Body — Date: _________ |

#### Purpose
To ensure that patients give voluntary, informed consent before any procedure or anesthesia administration, based on a clear understanding of the proposed treatment.

#### Policy Statement
Informed consent is obtained by the physician performing the procedure and/or the anesthesia provider. The informed consent discussion and the signed consent form are documented in the patient's medical record before the procedure begins.

#### Required Elements of Informed Consent

The informed consent discussion and form must include:

1. **Name of the procedure** in language the patient understands
2. **Name of the physician** performing the procedure
3. **Description of the procedure** in understandable terms
4. **Risks and potential complications** including those specific to the procedure
5. **Benefits of the procedure**
6. **Alternatives** to the proposed procedure, including the option of no treatment
7. **Type of anesthesia** to be used and associated risks
8. **Patient's acknowledgment** that they understand and agree to proceed
9. **Patient's signature** (or legal representative) with date and time
10. **Physician's signature** with date and time
11. **Witness signature** with date and time

#### Procedure
- Informed consent is obtained by the physician in a private setting, allowing adequate time for questions
- Consent is obtained BEFORE the patient receives any sedation or pre-operative medication that could impair judgment
- If the patient cannot give consent (minor, incapacitated), consent is obtained from the legal representative
- If an interpreter is used, this is documented on the consent form
- The original signed consent is placed in the patient's medical record
- Consent for the procedure AND consent for anesthesia are obtained (may be combined or separate forms per facility preference)

#### Special Circumstances
- **Emergency:** If an emergency arises during the procedure requiring a different or additional procedure, the physician documents the medical necessity
- **Refusal:** If a patient refuses consent, document the refusal, the information provided, and that the patient understands the consequences
- **Withdrawal:** A patient may withdraw consent at any time before the procedure

#### Purpose
To comply with the Patient Self-Determination Act by informing patients of their right to formulate advance directives and to establish a process for managing advance directives within the ASC setting.

#### Policy Statement
**[FACILITY NAME]** informs all patients of their right to formulate advance directives per state law. The facility documents whether the patient has an advance directive and, if so, obtains a copy for the medical record. In the ASC setting, the primary focus is safe completion of the planned procedure with transfer to an appropriate facility if the patient's condition deteriorates.

#### Procedure

1. During pre-operative assessment, staff asks each patient whether they have an advance directive
2. If yes: a copy is requested and placed in the medical record; the physician and anesthesia provider are informed
3. If no: the patient is provided with information about advance directives and resources to create one (the ASC does not create advance directives)
4. The physician discusses with the patient how the advance directive will be applied in the ASC setting — particularly that:
   - The primary goal is safe management during and after the procedure
   - Resuscitation and stabilization will be initiated in the event of an emergency during the procedure to safely complete the procedure
   - If a patient's condition deteriorates beyond the ASC's capability, transfer to a hospital will be initiated
5. The patient's wishes are documented and communicated to all members of the care team
6. If a patient wishes to suspend their advance directive during the procedure, this is documented

#### Purpose
To establish a process for receiving, investigating, and resolving patient complaints and grievances in a timely manner.

#### Definitions
- **Complaint:** A verbal or informal expression of dissatisfaction that can be resolved promptly at the point of service
- **Grievance:** A formal, written complaint or any complaint regarding abuse, neglect, patient harm, or Medicare billing that requires investigation

#### Procedure

**1. Receiving Complaints/Grievances**
- Any staff member may receive a complaint; all complaints are taken seriously
- Verbal complaints are documented and the staff member attempts immediate resolution
- If the complaint cannot be resolved immediately or the patient requests a formal process, it is escalated to a grievance
- Patients are informed of how to file a grievance (verbally and via posted notice)
- Grievance forms are available at the front desk and provided upon request

**2. Investigation**
- All grievances are logged in the Grievance Log with date received, patient name, nature of complaint, and assigned investigator
- Investigation is initiated within 3 business days
- The investigator interviews relevant staff, reviews medical records, and gathers facts
- Investigation is completed within 14 calendar days (or the patient is notified of the delay and expected resolution date)

**3. Resolution & Response**
- Written response to the patient includes: summary of investigation, actions taken, and who to contact for further concerns
- If the grievance involves quality of care, it is referred to QAPI committee
- If the grievance involves a physician, it is referred to the Medical Director

**4. External Reporting**
- Patients are informed of their right to file a complaint with:
  - The State Health Department: [STATE CONTACT INFO]
  - The accrediting body (AAAHC / Joint Commission / CMS): [ACCREDITOR CONTACT]
  - Medicare Beneficiary and Family Centered Care Quality Improvement Organization (BFCC-QIO): [QIO CONTACT]
- This information is posted in the facility and included in patient rights materials

**5. Tracking & Trending**
- All grievances are reviewed quarterly by the QAPI committee
- Trends are identified and systemic issues are addressed through performance improvement projects

#### Documentation
- Grievance log
- Investigation reports
- Written response letters
- QAPI committee minutes reflecting grievance review

## Policy 11: Malignant Hyperthermia Response

### [FACILITY NAME] — Policy & Procedure

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Policy Title** | Malignant Hyperthermia Response |
| **Policy Number** | EMRG-002 |
| **Department** | OR / Anesthesia |
| **CMS Reference** | 42 CFR §416.42(b) |
| **Effective Date** | [DATE] |
| **Last Reviewed** | [DATE] |
| **Next Review Due** | [DATE + 12 months] |
| **Approved By** | Governing Body — Date: _________ |

#### Purpose
To establish an immediate response protocol for malignant hyperthermia (MH) crisis events in the operating room.

#### Policy Statement
**[FACILITY NAME]** maintains an MH-specific response protocol, a dedicated MH cart/kit with Dantrolene, and trains all OR and anesthesia staff at least annually on MH recognition and response.

#### Procedure

**1. Recognition — Early Warning Signs**
- Unexplained rise in end-tidal CO2 (ETCO2)
- Unexplained tachycardia
- Muscle rigidity (especially masseter muscle rigidity after succinylcholine)
- Rising body temperature (may be a LATE sign)
- Metabolic acidosis, hyperkalemia
- Dark-colored urine (myoglobinuria)

**2. Immediate Response — Assign Roles**
- **Anesthesia Provider:** Discontinue all triggering agents immediately (volatile agents, succinylcholine); hyperventilate with 100% O2 at high flows; call for help; begin Dantrolene preparation
- **Circulating Nurse 1:** Bring MH cart to OR; call MHAUS Hotline: **1-800-644-9737**; begin mixing Dantrolene
- **Circulating Nurse 2 / Available Staff:** Obtain ice, cold saline; prepare cooling measures; draw labs (ABG, electrolytes, CK, myoglobin, coagulation panel, urine myoglobin)
- **Surgeon:** Conclude or abort procedure as rapidly as safely possible

**3. Dantrolene Administration**
- Initial dose: 2.5 mg/kg IV push
- Repeat 1 mg/kg every 5–10 minutes until symptoms resolve (no maximum dose in crisis)
- Mix each 20 mg vial with 60 mL sterile water (or per product formulation — check your supply)
- Continue Dantrolene for at least 24 hours post-crisis (1 mg/kg every 4–6 hours)

**4. Cooling Measures**
- Cold IV saline (NOT through blood warmer)
- Ice packs to groin, axillae, neck
- Cooling blanket if available
- Stop cooling at 38.5°C (101.3°F) to prevent overcooling

**5. Transfer**
- Initiate transfer to nearest hospital with ICU capability
- Notify receiving facility of MH crisis
- Send all documentation and lab results with patient

**6. MH Cart/Kit Contents & Checks**
- Dantrolene: minimum 36 vials (or per MHAUS guidelines for your facility's patient population)
- Sterile water for reconstitution
- Large-bore IV supplies, cold saline
- Cooling supplies
- Lab tubes for required blood draws
- MHAUS Hotline number posted on cart
- Cognitive aid / MH protocol checklist posted on cart
- Cart checked monthly (documented) for expiration dates and completeness

#### Policy Statement
All staff are trained in fire prevention and the RACE/PASS response protocol. Fire drills are conducted quarterly with documentation. Fire detection and suppression systems are maintained per NFPA standards.

#### Procedure

**1. Fire Prevention**
- No smoking on facility premises
- Electrical equipment is inspected per schedule; no unapproved personal appliances
- Extension cords are not used as permanent wiring
- Storage does not block electrical panels (36-inch clearance), sprinkler heads (18-inch clearance), or egress paths
- Oxygen storage follows NFPA 99 requirements
- Fire-resistant construction materials maintained

**2. Fire Response — RACE Protocol**
- **R — Rescue:** Remove patients/persons in immediate danger
- **A — Alarm:** Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station; call 911
- **C — Contain:** Close all doors to contain fire and smoke
- **E — Extinguish/Evacuate:** If small and contained, use fire extinguisher (PASS); if large, evacuate

**3. Fire Extinguisher Use — PASS**
- **P — Pull** the pin
- **A — Aim** at the base of the fire
- **S — Squeeze** the handle
- **S — Sweep** side to side

**4. Evacuation**
- Primary and secondary evacuation routes are posted in every department
- Patients in OR are stabilized before evacuation if possible; anesthesia provider manages airway
- Wheelchair and stretcher patients are evacuated per trained technique
- Assembly point: **[SPECIFY LOCATION]**
- Staff accountability is verified at assembly point

**5. Fire Drills**
- Conducted quarterly (minimum) on varying shifts and days
- At least one drill per year during hours of operation with patients present (or simulated)
- Documentation includes: date, time, shift, participants, scenario, response time, findings, corrective actions

**6. Fire System Maintenance**
- Fire alarm system: inspected and tested annually by qualified vendor
- Sprinkler system: inspected quarterly (visual) and annually (full inspection)
- Fire extinguishers: monthly visual inspection, annual professional inspection
- Emergency lighting: monthly 30-second test, annual 90-minute test
- Fire doors: inspected annually for proper operation

## Policy 16: Incident Reporting & Investigation

### [FACILITY NAME] — Policy & Procedure

| Field | Details |

| **Policy Title** | Incident Reporting & Investigation |
| **Policy Number** | QA-002 |
| **Department** | Facility-Wide |
| **CMS Reference** | 42 CFR §416.43 |
| **Effective Date** | [DATE] |
| **Last Reviewed** | [DATE] |
| **Next Review Due** | [DATE + 12 months] |
| **Approved By** | Governing Body — Date: _________ |

#### Purpose
To establish a standardized process for reporting, investigating, and resolving incidents, adverse events, and near-miss events to improve patient safety and prevent recurrence.

#### Definitions
- **Incident:** Any event that is not consistent with routine care or operations that did or could result in harm
- **Adverse Event:** An incident that results in patient harm
- **Near-Miss:** An event that could have resulted in harm but was caught before reaching the patient
- **Sentinel Event:** An unexpected occurrence involving death or serious physical/psychological injury

#### Procedure

**1. Reporting**
- Any staff member who witnesses or becomes aware of an incident must complete an incident report within 24 hours
- Reports are submitted to the Administrator / Risk Manager
- Reporting is non-punitive — the focus is system improvement, not individual blame
- Near-misses are reported using the same process

**2. Immediate Response**
- Ensure patient safety and provide necessary treatment
- Notify the attending physician immediately
- Preserve any physical evidence
- Document factual observations in the medical record (NOT the incident report)
- The incident report is NOT part of the medical record

**3. Investigation**
- All incidents are reviewed by the Administrator within 48 hours
- Serious events or sentinel events trigger a root cause analysis (RCA)
- Investigation includes: timeline of events, contributing factors, interviews, record review, system analysis
- RCA identifies root causes (not just proximate causes) and recommends corrective actions

**4. Corrective Actions**
- Corrective action plans are developed for all identified root causes
- Actions are specific, measurable, and assigned to a responsible party with a deadline
- Effectiveness of corrective actions is monitored

**5. External Reporting**
- Sentinel events are reported per state requirements and accreditor requirements
- Device-related adverse events are reported to FDA MedWatch
- Reportable infections are reported to the state health department

**6. Trending & QAPI Integration**
- All incidents are logged and trended monthly
- Incident data is reviewed by QAPI committee quarterly
- Patterns trigger performance improvement projects

#### Policy Statement
No patient is discharged without meeting established discharge criteria, receiving a physician's discharge order, and receiving written discharge instructions. A responsible adult must be available to accompany the patient who has received sedation or anesthesia.

#### Discharge Criteria

Patients must meet the following criteria before discharge (based on modified Aldrete scoring or facility-equivalent):

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Level of consciousness | Alert and oriented (or returned to pre-procedure baseline) |
| Vital signs | Stable for minimum 30 minutes; within acceptable parameters |
| SpO2 | ≥ 92% on room air (or at pre-procedure baseline) |
| Pain | Controlled to an acceptable level (patient-reported) |
| Nausea/Vomiting | Controlled or absent |
| Surgical site | No active bleeding; dressing dry and intact |
| Ambulation | Able to ambulate with minimal assistance (or at baseline) |
| Voiding | Per facility/physician criteria based on procedure type |
| Responsible adult | Present and identified |

#### Procedure

**1. Discharge Assessment**
- Registered nurse assesses patient against discharge criteria
- Physician authorizes discharge (written or verbal order)
- Any deviation from standard discharge criteria is documented with physician justification

**2. Discharge Instructions**
Written discharge instructions are provided and reviewed with the patient AND responsible adult, including:
- Procedure performed and name of physician
- Post-operative care instructions specific to the procedure
- Activity restrictions and duration
- Diet instructions
- Medication instructions (new prescriptions, changes to home medications)
- Pain management plan
- Signs and symptoms requiring immediate medical attention
- When and how to contact the surgeon
- Follow-up appointment information
- Emergency contact numbers (facility, surgeon, 911)

**3. Documentation**
- Patient and responsible adult sign acknowledgment of receiving discharge instructions
- Copy of instructions given to patient; copy filed in medical record
- Discharge time, condition at discharge, mode of transport, and responsible adult documented

**4. Post-Discharge Follow-Up**
- **[FACILITY NAME]** conducts a post-discharge follow-up contact within 24–72 hours
- Follow-up assesses: pain level, signs of infection or complications, adherence to instructions, patient satisfaction
- If the patient reports a concern, the surgeon is notified and appropriate action is taken
- Follow-up call is documented in the patient's medical record
