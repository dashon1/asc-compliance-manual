# ASC Compliance Manual

## Ambulatory Surgery Center Compliance & Accreditation System

**Version:** 1.0  
**Target Audience:** Small ASC administrators and compliance staff (1–2 person compliance teams)  
**Applicable Standards:** CMS Conditions for Coverage (42 CFR Part 416), AAAHC, Joint Commission, OSHA, HIPAA, State Licensure

---

## Why This Product Exists

Losing Medicare certification is catastrophic for an Ambulatory Surgery Center. A single failed survey can shut your doors. Yet small ASCs face an impossible challenge: maintaining compliance across dozens of federal, state, and accrediting body requirements with only 1–2 people who also carry full clinical workloads.

This manual is built specifically for that reality. Every document is designed to be actionable, not theoretical — ready to implement, track, and present to surveyors.

## How to Use This Manual

### Getting Started (Tier 1 Buyers)

1. **Start with the Accreditation Roadmap** — Identify which month of the 12-month cycle aligns with your current compliance status. If you're starting from scratch, begin at Month 1. If you're mid-cycle, jump to the relevant month and work forward.

2. **Set up the Equipment Maintenance Log** — Complete the master inventory for every clinical device in your facility. This is often a one-day project. Once set up, monthly maintenance takes only 30 minutes.

3. **Schedule your first Mock Survey** — Use the Survey Readiness Checklist to conduct a comprehensive self-assessment. This is the single most impactful thing you can do. The first mock will reveal your true compliance status.

### Expanding to Tier 2

4. **Build your Training System** — Use the Staff Training Checklist to identify which staff need which training, when. Create a 12-month training calendar and start closing gaps.

5. **Customize your Policies** — Work through the Policy Template Library one policy at a time. Start with the highest-risk areas: Infection Control, Credentialing, and Emergency Preparedness.

6. **Implement Quarterly Reviews** — Use the Quarterly Update Guide to establish the habit of continuous compliance. The quarterly self-audit is your early warning system.
