# Staff Training Checklist

## Complete Training Matrix for ASC Compliance Roles

**Product:** Ambulatory Surgery Center Compliance Manual — Tier 2  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Applicable Standards:** CMS §416.46 (Staffing & Staff Responsibilities), OSHA, AAAHC Staffing Standards, State Requirements

---

## Purpose

This document provides a comprehensive training matrix covering every required and recommended training topic for ASC staff. It is designed for small operations where 1–2 people manage compliance alongside clinical duties.

### How to Use
1. **Complete the Staff Roster** (Section 1) with all employees and their roles
2. **Use the Master Training Matrix** (Section 2) to identify required training per role
3. **Track completion** using the Individual Training Records (Section 3)
4. **Follow the Training Calendar** (Section 4) for scheduling
5. **Use the Competency Validation Tools** (Section 5) to document skills assessment

### Training Day Logistics for Small Teams

> **Reality Check for Small ASCs:** You cannot shut down for a full training day. Here's how to make it work:

1. **Stagger training** — Train half the staff one week, the other half the next
2. **Use pre-work** — Send online modules before the in-person session to reduce classroom time
3. **Lunch-and-learn format** — 30–60 minute focused sessions during lunch breaks
4. **Combine topics** — Group related training (e.g., all OSHA topics in one session)
5. **Document EVERYTHING** — Sign-in sheets, pre/post tests, competency checklists. If it isn't documented, it didn't happen
6. **Provider training** — Physicians may not attend staff meetings. Create a physician-specific packet and obtain their signature acknowledging receipt and completion
