# Survey Readiness Checklist

## Mock Survey & Inspector Walkthrough Simulation

**Product:** Ambulatory Surgery Center Compliance Manual — Tier 1  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Applicable Standards:** CMS Conditions for Coverage (42 CFR Part 416), AAAHC Standards, State Licensure Requirements

---

## Purpose

This document simulates an official accreditation survey. Use it to conduct internal mock surveys that replicate the methodology, sequencing, and scrutiny of CMS and AAAHC surveyors. Identify deficiencies before the real survey team does.

### How Real Surveys Work

| Survey Element | CMS Survey | AAAHC Survey |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Notice** | Unannounced (typically) | Scheduled (60–90 day window) |
| **Duration** | 1–2 days | 1–2 days |
| **Team Size** | 1–3 surveyors | 1–2 surveyors (peer-based) |
| **Method** | Tracer methodology (follow the patient) | Standards-based with tracer elements |
| **Focus Areas** | Patient safety, infection control, surgical services, environment | Quality, governance, clinical records, patient rights |
| **Outcome** | Compliance / Deficiency citations | Accreditation / Recommendations |

### Mock Survey Instructions

1. **Schedule a full day** — block OR cases if possible
2. **Assign a "surveyor" role** — ideally someone NOT involved in daily operations (consultant, colleague from another facility, or your most detail-oriented staff member)
3. **Follow this checklist sequentially** — it mirrors the actual survey flow
4. **Document every finding** — use the scoring system below
5. **Photograph deficiencies** — visual evidence drives accountability
6. **Complete the Summary Report** at the end (Section 10)

### Scoring System

| Score | Meaning | Action Required |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| ✅ **Compliant** | Meets all requirements | Document as compliant; maintain |
| ⚠️ **Partial** | Minor gap; easily correctable | Corrective action within 30 days |
| ❌ **Non-Compliant** | Significant gap; would be cited | Immediate corrective action required |
| 🔴 **Immediate Jeopardy** | Patient safety risk | Stop operations if necessary; correct immediately |

## Section 9: Staff Interview Preparation

*Surveyors will interview staff at all levels. They ask questions designed to determine whether staff understand their roles, the facility's policies, and how to respond to emergencies. Prepare your team with these common questions.*

### 9.1 Questions for ALL Clinical Staff

| # | Question | Expected Response Elements | Staff Member Tested | Adequate? |
|---|----------|--------------------------|--------------------|-----------| 
| 1 | "What do you do if there's a fire?" | RACE: Rescue, Alarm, Contain, Extinguish/Evacuate. PASS: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep. Know fire alarm pull station location. Know evacuation route. | | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| 2 | "Where is the crash cart and how do you activate the emergency response?" | Location, how to call for help, BLS/ACLS role, who leads the code | | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| 3 | "What are the steps for surgical site identification?" | Time-out procedure, two patient identifiers, surgical site marking, Universal Protocol | | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| 4 | "How do you handle a blood/body fluid exposure?" | Immediate wound care, report to supervisor, incident report, post-exposure prophylaxis process | | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| 5 | "What is your hand hygiene policy?" | 5 moments of hand hygiene, soap vs. sanitizer, duration, when gloves are required | | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| 6 | "How do you report a patient complaint or grievance?" | Process for receiving, documenting, and escalating complaints; who to contact | | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| 7 | "What is your role in infection control?" | Personal responsibilities, reporting infections/exposures, standard precautions | | ☐ Yes ☐ No |
| 8 | "How do you identify a patient before medication administration?" | Two identifiers (name + DOB), allergy check, right patient-right drug-right dose-right route-right time | | ☐ Yes ☐ No |

### 9.4 Interview Coaching Tips for Small Teams

1. **Don't memorize scripts** — surveyors can tell. Understand the *why* behind each policy
2. **It's OK to say "I'd look it up in our policy manual"** — surveyors want to know you know WHERE to find the answer
3. **Never say "I don't know" and leave it there** — always add what you WOULD do ("I would contact my supervisor" or "I would reference our emergency protocol binder")
4. **Be honest** — if you haven't done a fire drill this quarter, don't lie. It's worse to be caught in a false statement
5. **Show your documentation** — if a surveyor asks about infection control, walk them to your IC binder. Physical demonstration builds confidence
6. **Every staff member matters** — housekeeping, front desk, and clinical staff are all fair game for interviews
